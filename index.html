Theoretical Antecedents

Research Questions

  • Participation in social movements have an strong emotional component, related to the motivational process of participation.
  • Accordingly, many social psychological models include emotion as predictor of participation in collective actions
  • On the other hand, more and more soial movements are using social media to communicate. This wealth of data constains the expressed emotions of the social movements participants/sympathizers.
  • I investigated the dynamic expression of four emotions in the tweets associated with three social movements.
  • I analysed the relation of the expression of these four emotions with the real-life events and collective actions performed by each of these social movements.

Which Emotions and Why

  • In this work I chose four emotions to detect in the tweets: fear, anger, sadness and joy.
  • This is a departure from previous research on social media emotions that usually use 6 to 8 emotions.
  • I support the use of four emotions with recent evidence from neuropsychology sugesting that fear, anger, sadness and joy are emotions basic enough to be present in every culture and even in organisms as simple as flies.

Theoretical Inspiration

  • This work is mainly based on socio psychological models of collective action participation.
  • In particular, the main source of inspiration was the model proposed by Becker and Tausch (2015).

Main Findings

  • Anger is the main emotion in the Twitter data of all movements. However, is not statistically related to the collective actions.
  • There seem to be a feedback loop in the reports of collective actions. This might have practical implications.
  • Fear was the second most important emotion for the Hong Kong and Fridays for Future movements, but its relation to events is also weak.
  • Joy and sadness only had significant effects on particular cases in the Chilean Social Movement.

Conclusion

Contributions

  • I propose a framework to analyse social media data of social movements that includes emotions/target pairs.
  • I propose the use of a limited set of emotions for the study of emotions in text.
  • I provide a way to link expresion of emotion in social media to contention events in real life. This could have policy implications.

Future Challenges

  • Research in better ways to identify the genral aspects of social media, hopefully in an automatic way.
  • Research in which I can include more contectual information in the classification of emotions to reduce the changes of misclassification.
  • Aplication of this framework in a large set of social movements.
